Cipher Bloom — Reflection
=========================

> *What should be public, what should stay hidden, and what should be decided by
> AI versus by a human in a bounty system?*

The rules of the garden should be fully public — the brief, the rubric, the
reward, the deadlines, and the sealed shell of every entry — because a contest is
only trustworthy if anyone can audit it afterward. The seeds themselves must stay
hidden while planting is open; a visible answer lets a latecomer copy the first
good idea and quietly win a race they never really ran. Once the planting window
closes, the answers can safely open into public blooms (commit-reveal) or, when a
bounty needs more, stay encrypted and be judged privately inside a TEE. Reading
every eligible bloom at once and ranking them against a rubric is exactly the
kind of repetitive, consistent work an AI does well and a human dreads, so the
machine should do the evaluation. But the AI should only ever recommend: language
models can be prompt-injected, can invent scores, and answer to no one when money
moves. The final pick and the payout belong to a human owner, who can check the
ranking against the rubric, reject a gamed result, and stand behind the outcome.
Keep the rules open, keep the contents sealed until the season turns, let the AI
scale the reading, and let a person own the harvest.
